About Us

Who we are

We are a group of bisexual survivors facilitating peer-lead,
online support groups for survivors of sexual and/or
domestic violence/abuse.

We are also advocates for bisexual, pansexual, and other
non-monosexual (bi+) survivors, working to ensure that
our voices are heard and our needs met.

What we do

We run bi-weekly chats via the secure messaging app Telegram.

For each session, we set a theme (such as healing tools,
finding closure, dealing with flashbacks) as a way of
shapeing the conversation, but participants are free to discuss
anything relevant.

Often, people find it very useful and healing just to have a space
to finally share what happened to them and have others validate
the pain that it has caused.

Each chat session is moderated by one or more members
of the BSN team.

The channels are private, which means you need to be invited
by a link and other people can’t find the chat and join it.

After the session is finished, a moderatore deletes the channel,
leaving no traces of the discussion.

You are more than welcome to use a screen name when joining
if you want to be anonymous.

Why we do it

Sexual and domestic violence is one of the biggest issues
facing the bi+ community.

Bi+ women face higher rates of sexual/domestic violence
than gay and straight women, and report worse experiences
when seeking help or support.
Bi+ men also face higher rates of sexual violence than monosexual men.

We are currently the only bi+ specific resource for survivors in the UK.

Many bi+ people don’t feel comfortable using mainstream
survivor support services because they may have to
out themselves or experience homophobia/biphobia.

They often do not feel comfortable accessing
LGBTQIA+ survivor support services because of
the prevalence of biphobia among the LGBTQIA+ community.

They also, rightfully, do not feel that these resources
will have the knowledge or understanding of bi+ issues
necessary to support them.

Part of our advocacy of bi+ survivors is challenging the biphobia
that leaves bi+ people so vulnerable to sexual/domestic violence,
and isolates us from support.
